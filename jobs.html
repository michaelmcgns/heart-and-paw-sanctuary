Veterinary
Veterinary Nurse
Full-time On-site · Siena €28,000–€34,000

We are looking for a qualified veterinary nurse to join our on-site medical team. You will assist with daily health checks, post-operative care, vaccination programmes, and the ongoing medical management of our 40–60 resident dogs.

Requirements
  • Qualified veterinary nurse (RVN or European equivalent)
  • Minimum 2 years clinical experience
  • Experience with dogs essential; rescue/shelter experience preferred
  • Italian or English essential; both a strong advantage
  • Genuine passion for animal welfare

Animal Care
Kennel Manager
Full-time or Part-time On-site · Siena €26,000–€32,000

Oversee the daily running of our kennel and outdoor enclosure areas across the sanctuary's 8 hectares. You will lead a small care team, manage feeding and enrichment schedules, coordinate with our vet on resident health, and ensure every dog's environment meets the highest welfare standards.

Requirements
  • Proven experience in kennel, shelter, or rescue management
  • Strong leadership and team coordination skills
  • Deep knowledge of canine behaviour and welfare standards
  • Physical fitness — the role involves outdoor work in all conditions
  • Italian or English essential; both a strong advantage

Animal Care
Kennel Assistant
Full-time or Part-time On-site · Siena €18,000–€22,000

A hands-on entry-level role supporting the Kennel Manager with the day-to-day care of our resident dogs. You will feed, walk, clean, and — most importantly — spend time with dogs who need human connection to heal. No two days are the same.

Requirements
  • A genuine love for dogs and calm, patient energy around animals
  • Physical fitness and willingness to work outdoors in all weather
  • Previous experience with dogs is helpful but not essential
  • Reliable, punctual, and a strong team contributor
  • Basic Italian or English; willingness to learn on the job

Operations · Remote
International Assistant
Full-time or Part-time Remote Online €20,000–€26,000

A fully remote role supporting our international adoption and fostering network. You will be the first point of contact for families across Europe — responding to enquiries, coordinating transport logistics, translating communications, and ensuring every family has a smooth experience from first message to adoption day.

Requirements
  • Fluency in English plus at least one other European language (German, Dutch, French, or Spanish strongly preferred)
  • Excellent written communication and customer-facing skills
  • Organised, self-motivated, and comfortable working independently
  • Familiarity with EU pet transport and import/export requirements a strong advantage
  • Genuine passion for animal welfare and the Heart & Paw mission
